About Me

Post-doctoral researcher at the Hamburg University (Germany), working at the Hamburg Observatory. I like to observe non-thermal phenomena in radio galaxies and galaxy clusters at different radio wavelenghts.

Together with this, I also work in HPC and GPU enabling of softwares for radio interferometric imaging.

I love sports (huge football and tennis fan), music, and crime novels.

Skills

Programming languages and interfaces

  • C/C++, Python, Bash, CWL, Fortran, IDL, definetly *not* HTML (as you can easily guess from this website...)

  • MPI, OpenMP, CUDA, HIP, for parallelization and GPU offloading on multiple architectures; NVIDIA HPC-SDK, ROCm, and profiling tools (such as NVIDIA Nsight)

  • Wide expertise in working with high-level HPC clusters, such as Leonardo (CINECA), Setonix (Pawsey) and Pleiadi (INAF), and SLURM job management

Software

  • Developer of Radio Imaging Code Kernels (RICK), to parallelize and accelerate algorithms for interferometric imaging

  • LOFAR data reduction pipelines (LINC/Prefactor, DDFpipeline, LOFAR-VLBI pipeline) and dependent softwares (DP3, WSClean)

  • CASA data calibration and imaging using VLA data (both total intensity and polarization)

  • Capture and SPAM pipeline for uGMRT
